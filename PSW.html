Platform Security Tips

Be Cautious with Pop-Ups and Unexpected Prompts

Avoid entering credentials or personal information into pop-up windows or unexpected login prompts. Instead, navigate directly to the official site.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Add an extra layer of security by enabling two-factor authentication (2FA) on your accounts whenever possible.

Monitor and Log Activities

Continuously monitor system logs and user activities to detect suspicious behavior early.

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, applications, and security software to patch vulnerabilities.

Use Secure Connections

Always use HTTPS websites and avoid logging into sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi without a VPN.

Backup Your Data

Keep regular backups of important files in case of malware attacks or accidental loss.

Regularly Review Account Activity

Monitor your bank, email, and other important accounts for unauthorized or suspicious activity.

Limit App Permissions

Review and restrict unnecessary permissions for apps to protect your personal data from misuse.

Log Out from Shared Devices

Always log out of accounts when using public or shared devices to prevent unauthorized access.

Use Antivirus Protection

Install and regularly update antivirus software to detect and block malware threats.

Be Careful with Downloads

Download apps and files only from trusted sources to avoid installing harmful software.

Secure Your Devices

Set up screen locks, PINs, or biometrics on your devices to prevent unauthorized physical access.
